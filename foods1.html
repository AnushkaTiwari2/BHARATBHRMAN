FOOD

APPAM

Appam

Appam is a Keralan staple made from fermented rice flour and coconut milk. The addition of aromatic whole spices, ginger and fresh coconut milk enhance the natural flavor of the vegetables.

ERISSERY

Erissery

This is a popular curry in Kerala, made from either raw plantains or sliced yams. It appears on most menus at religious festivals like Onam.

KARIMEEN

Karimeen

Karimeen or pearl spot fish is a speckled fish commonly found in the backwaters of this state.

PRAWN CURRY

Prawn Curry

This is a signature dish of the state - a prawn curry from the Malabar region made with a blend of fenugreek, black mustard and fennel seeds, coconut milk and green chilli.

MALABAR

Malabar

Layered flat bread that originated in the Malabar region called Parotta. This is then eaten with beef curry (pieces of beef simmered in a traditional curry).

PUTTU CURRY

Puttu curry

This is a breakfast staple eaten all over the state. Puttu is a cylindrical steamed rice cake cooked in a mould with grated coconut and is usually served with kadala curry.

THALASSERY BIRYANI

Thalassery biryani

Thalassery biryani uses a unique, fragrant, small-grained, thin rice variety named kaima. The biryani masala and cooked rice are arranged in layers inside the dish.

PALADA

Palada

Payasam prepared with palada (rice ada) is common sweet delicacy prepared in almost all Kerala households during festival of Onam and many other occasions.
